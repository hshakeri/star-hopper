# Parents' Guide to Star Hopper Space Laboratory 🚀

Welcome to the Star Hopper Flight Deck! Star Hopper is not just a game; it is a **programmable physics playground** disguised as a space platformer. Your child is stepping into the shoes of a Space Flight Dynamics Engineer. 

Here is what you need to know to help them learn, explore, and build confidence—even if you have never written a line of code in your life!

---

## 🔬 What is My Child Learning?
1. **Newtonian Mechanics**: How gravity governs falling speed, how friction slows things down, and how energy changes form.
2. **Energy Conservation**: Witnessing potential energy (height) transform into kinetic energy (speed) in real-time.
3. **Coding Logic**: Writing instructions in KidCode, a friendly programming language, to change physics constants, write conditional checks (`if`), and program loop sequences.
4. **The Scientific Method**: Following a structured loop of observing a problem, making predictions, running code experiments, and reflecting on the results.

---

## 👨‍👩‍👧 The 15-Minute "Space Coach" Routine
You do not need to be a physics professor to help. Use these four prompts to guide your child through any level:

1. **Observe (Before Coding)**: 
   * *"What is happening to Star or Hopper right now? Why can't they reach that platform?"*
2. **Predict (Before Running)**: 
   * *"If we lower gravity from 0.6 to 0.2, what do you think will happen to our jump height? What about our falling speed?"*
3. **Code (During Play)**: 
   * *"Type that instruction into the Space Terminal. Is the spelling exactly correct?"*
4. **Reflect (After Testing)**: 
   * *"Why did Star stay in the air so long? Did that match your prediction?"*

## 📚 Key Space Science Terms to Use Together
* **Gravity ($g$)**: The invisible pulling force between objects. Higher gravity pulls you down faster; zero gravity lets you float.
* **Friction ($\mu$)**: The resistance force when two surfaces slide past each other. Ice has low friction (slippery); ground has high friction (sticky).
* **Kinetic Energy ($KE$)**: Energy of motion. The faster the ship or character moves, the higher their kinetic energy.
* **Potential Energy ($PE$)**: Stored energy based on height. The higher the platform, the more potential energy they have.
* **Velocity ($v$)**: Speed in a specific direction (horizontal $X$ or vertical $Y$).
