# 🏫 STAR HOPPER CLASSROOM LESSON PLAN

**Target Grade Levels:** 3-8 (Ages 8-14)  
**Subject Focus:** Physics (Forces & Motion, Energy Conservation) & Computational Thinking

---

## 🎯 Educational Standards Mapping

### Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)
*   **MS-PS2-2 (Forces & Motion):** Plan an investigation to show how motion changes depending on net force and mass.  
    *Alignment:* Comparing light-weight Star (mass 1.0) with heavy Hopper (mass 2.5) using rocket boosts.
*   **MS-PS3-2 (Potential/Kinetic Energy):** Develop models to describe energy stored and transferred in gravity fields.  
    *Alignment:* Reading real-time potential and kinetic energy gauges during platformer runs.

### Computer Science Standards (CSTA)
*   **1B-AP-10 / 2-AP-12 (Control Structures):** Create programs including sequences, loops, events, and conditionals.  
    *Alignment:* Writing KidCode commands (`repeat`, `if`, `when` triggers) to manipulate simulation parameters.

---

## ⏱️ 45-Minute Classroom Agenda

*   **00m - 10m: Introduction & Demonstration**
    *   Introduce concepts of gravity ($g$), friction ($\mu$), and potential/kinetic energy transfers.
    *   Demo how to write `gravity = 0.2` in the Earth base camp.
*   **10m - 30m: Lab Exploration**
    *   Students work individually or in pairs (Pilot + Flight Dynamics Engineer).
    *   Complete Earth, Moon, Jupiter, and Glacies levels.
    *   Task: Record at least **3 completed reflections** in the Mission Log.
*   **30m - 40m: Spaceship Keplerian Orbit Challenge**
    *   Use the spacecraft story bridge in Play, or open advanced orbit tools for extension work.
    *   Students execute circular orbit thrust commands (`point_at('earth'); thrust(3.5, 1.5)`).
*   **40m - 45m: Academy Graduation**
    *   Verify missions, print certificates, and export local JSON saves to submit work.

---

## 📝 Classroom Inquiry Assessment Questions

1.  *Where in a character's jump arc is Potential Energy (PE) at its absolute maximum? Where is Kinetic Energy (KE) largest?*
2.  *Why does the heavy Hopper character fall much faster and jump lower than Star when using identical gravity?* (Hint: Resistance to acceleration is proportional to mass).
3.  *What happens to the spaceship trajectory vector when engine thrust is fired perpendicular to gravity vs. directly parallel?*
