# 🚀 STAR HOPPER PARENT LAB CARD

Welcome to the Star Hopper Flight Deck! Star Hopper is a programmable physics playground where children learn coding and science by modifying the laws of physics.

---

## 🔬 Core Vocabulary to Use Together

*   **Gravity ($g$):** The downward pulling force. Lower gravity lets you jump higher and float; higher gravity pulls you down quickly.
*   **Mass ($m$):** How hard an object is to accelerate. More mass needs more force to speed up or jump, but it does not make free fall faster under the same gravity.
*   **Friction ($\mu$):** The resistance force that opposes sliding. Ice has low friction (slippery); earth/dirt has high friction (sticky).
*   **Kinetic Energy ($KE$):** Energy of movement. Fast speeds produce high Kinetic Energy.
*   **Potential Energy ($PE$):** Stored energy based on height. High platforms store high Potential Energy.

---

## 💬 The 15-Minute Coaching Routine
You do not need to know code to support your child! Guide them through any challenge using these four prompts:

1.  **Observe:** *"Why can't Star or Hopper reach that high platform right now? What is stopping them?"*
2.  **Predict:** *"If we reduce the gravity pull to 0.2, what do you think will happen to our jump altitude?"*
3.  **Code:** *"Let's program that parameter in the space terminal. Is the command spelled exactly correct?"*
4.  **Reflect:** *"Look at the real-time energy telemetry. Did the jump height match your prediction?"*

---

## 💻 Cool Coding Recipes to Try

Type these instructions into the **Code** tab console and press **Enter**:

*   `gravity = 0.15` (Float like an astronaut on the Space Station!)
*   `friction = 12` (Walk on slippery ice slopes with high traction boots!)
*   `repeat 3: spawn_spring()` (Spawn a series of high-bounce spring cushions!)
*   `when player.lands: gravity = 1.8` (Trigger heavy gravity spikes immediately when landing!)
