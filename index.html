🚀 STAR HOPPER PARENT LAB CARD
🔬 Key Vocabulary
  • Gravity (g): The pull force between objects. Lower gravity lets you jump higher and fall slower.
  • Friction (μ): Resistance against sliding. Slippery ice has low friction; sticky ground has high friction.
  • Kinetic Energy (KE): Energy of motion. Fast speeds produce high KE.
  • Potential Energy (PE): Stored height energy. High platforms have high PE.
💬 The 15-Minute Coaching Routine

1. Observe: "What is blocking Star/Hopper right now? Why can't they jump that high?"

2. Predict: "If we cut gravity in half, what do you think will happen to our jumps?"

4. Reflect: "Look at the telemetry. Did the jump match your prediction?"

🏫 STAR HOPPER CLASSROOM LESSON PLAN
🎯 Lesson Objectives

Students will design and run simulations to demonstrate gravity and mass relationships (NGSS MS-PS2-2), describe potential/kinetic energy conservation loops (NGSS MS-PS3-1/2), and build algorithmic structures (loops, events, conditionals) in a sandboxed terminal (CSTA 1B-AP-10/11).

⏱️ 45-Minute Lab Agenda
00m - 10mIntro: Define gravity, friction, potential/kinetic energy. Demo writing gravity = 0.2.
10m - 30mExploration: Students complete Earth, Moon, Jupiter, and Glacies levels, recording reflections.
30m - 40mOrbit Challenge: Navigate a circular trajectory around Earth using thrust vectors.
40m - 45mWrap-up: Students export logs, print certificates, and submit reflections.
📝 Assessment Questions
  1. Where in the jump is Potential Energy (PE) highest? Where is Kinetic Energy (KE) highest?
  2. Why does the heavy character Hopper require more force than Star to jump the same height?
  3. What happens to a sliding crate if friction is set to zero?
